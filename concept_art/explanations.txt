Technical Implementation Note


All sprites follow the Hi-Res 2D Hand-Drawn style. When importing into Godot, ensure Texture Filter is set to Linear to preserve the smooth gradients required for realistic Lantern Engine light interaction.

Technical Placement Logic for OpenClaw

    Z-Index: Environmental assets should be placed on Layer 0 (Ground) and Layer 1 (Obstacles).

    Occlusion Culling: Each asset requires a LightOccluder2D polygon drawn to match its silhouette to ensure realistic shadow casting.

    Navigation: NavigationRegion2D must be baked around these assets to allow enemy AI to pathfind through the ruins.

dungeon_crawl_nature.png

    Theme: "The Black Timber" – Post-collapse biological overgrowth.

    Visual Narrative: Depicts the Scavenger navigating a dense, distorted forest. The trees are gnarled, partially charred, and alien in their growth patterns, suggesting a world where nature has reclaimed civilization in a violent manner. Rusting industrial debris is scattered among the roots.

    Technical Role (Lantern Engine): Used to test Foliage Occlusion. The goal is to demonstrate how the flashlight beam interacts with irregular, organic shapes, creating a "God Ray" effect through the mist and branches.

    Scale: Medium-shot. Focuses on the claustrophobia of the woods.



dungeon_crawl_city.png

    Theme: "The Silent Boulevard" – Urban desolation and infrastructure decay.

    Visual Narrative: A wide-angle view of a massive, ruined city street. The Scavenger is depicted as a small, vulnerable figure against the backdrop of multi-story, crumbling skyscrapers and collapsed facades. Abandoned vehicles and downed power lines litter the cracked asphalt.

    Technical Role (Lantern Engine): Used to test Hard-Surface Occlusion and Multi-Source Lighting. The scene features the primary flashlight beam interacting with large-scale geometric structures (buildings) and secondary environmental light sources, such as flickering electrical sparks from a damaged terminal.

    Scale: Cinematic Wide-shot. Establishes the true scale of the world and the vastness of the "City Dungeon" biome.

Implementation Directives for OpenClaw
1. Scale & Proportion

    Rule: Always maintain the character-to-environment ratio established in dungeon_crawl_city_v2.png. The world must feel "oppressively large" to enhance the survival-horror element.

2. Atmospheric Rendering

    Mist/Fog: Use the CanvasItem.material to implement a light-reactive fog. The fog should only be visible when illuminated by the LanternLight2D or environmental sparks.

    Shadow Softness: Shadows cast by the buildings in the city biome should have a higher shadow_filter_smooth value than the sharp, jagged shadows in the forest.
